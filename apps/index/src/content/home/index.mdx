  faqs:
    - question: "What does the Governance Benchmark measure?"
      answer: "Four first-order behavioral dimensions of an AI model under realistic open-book governance inputs: Evidence Confinement (does it stay within the source material it was given?), Source Attribution Stability (does it cite the right sources, stably?), Instruction Fidelity (does it follow the open-book instruction?), and Abstention/Uncertainty Calibration (does it decline when the evidence doesn't support an answer?). Plus a cross-cutting reliability profile and a structural audit of any governance payload the model emits."
    - question: "Is the benchmark open source?"
      answer: "Yes. The methodology, the reference runner, and the probe registry are all published under Apache 2.0. Reports are cryptographically signed and verifiable by any third party using the open-source `kgb verify` command. Anyone can re-run the exact published seeds against any model API and produce a signed replay report."
    - question: "Who runs the leaderboard?"
      answer: "Kenshiki Labs publishes and operates the public Index. Crucially, Kenshiki Labs is not the judge of any single evaluation — the judge ensemble is external (two pinned frontier models from different provider families, plus a human-annotator calibration sample). Anti-conflict routing prevents a judge from scoring its own provider family. When Kenshiki's own runtime product enters the Index, results require third-party replay confirmation before publication."
    - question: "How is the benchmark different from MMLU or HaluEval?"
      answer: "MMLU measures capability. HaluEval, FACTS Grounding, and SimpleQA measure factuality. KGB measures process properties — whether the model's reasoning can be governed. It is to AI governance what HaluEval is to factuality: a public, methodology-open, continuously-published yardstick the industry can argue about."
    - question: "Is a KGB report a compliance certificate?"
      answer: "No. A KGB report is evidence that can support a compliance audit. It measures process properties under a specific probe suite. It is not itself a regulatory filing or a certification by any standards body. Vendors using KGB reports to support regulatory submissions remain responsible for the underlying compliance."

## Governance has had no shared yardstick

Hallucination has HaluEval, FACTS Grounding, SimpleQA. Capability has MMLU and HELM. Procurement teams ask vendors "does your model stay within evidence?" and get marketing answers. Regulators ask "is this model auditable?" and get principles documents.

**The Governance Benchmark closes that gap.** A public, methodology-open, continuously-published benchmark that measures whether a model's reasoning can be governed — whether it stays within evidence, attributes correctly, follows instructions, and abstains when it should. Open source. Apache 2.0. Reproducible builds. Cryptographically signed reports.
